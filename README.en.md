**Not magnetic. It's vibrational.**

Osteoporosis affects millions of people, especially postmenopausal women. Bones become fragile, increasing the risk of fractures from falls or minor impacts. Current treatments rely on drugs (with side effects) or weight-bearing exercises (which not everyone can perform).

OsteoFlux explores a non-invasive alternative: mechanical resonance (vibration).

## What it does

The project proposes an architecture that, based on accurate bone density diagnosis (via DXA or REMS), applies specific vibration frequencies to stimulate bone tissue stabilization and recovery.

The system is based on the physical principle that every material (including bone) has characteristic resonance frequencies. Healthy and osteoporotic bone vibrate differently. OsteoFlux identifies those frequencies and applies controlled vibrations to promote regeneration.

## Medical and legal aspects

OsteoFlux is an applied research project. It is not a medical device. It has not been clinically validated. It is not offered as a treatment or as a replacement for established therapies.

**Use of certified components**: The vibration actuators, sensors, and power supplies used in the prototype are commercial components with CE, FCC, or RoHS certification. The project does not manufacture these components, only integrates them.

**No medical certification required**: As it is not a medical device nor offered as such, ISO 13485 or IEC 60601 regulations do not apply. The project is shared for educational and research purposes.
